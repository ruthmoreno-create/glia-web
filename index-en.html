A study session with Glia

From “I can’t” to “ok… I’m on it”

It’s not magic. It’s design for when your brain is overloaded.

1) “I have a PDF… and zero motivation”

You don’t start because the material is overwhelming. With Glia, you upload the PDF or a photo. The app turns it into something you can actually look at: clear options.

📥 Import PDF / photo 🧠 Less friction 🧩 Start with something small
Import → choose → done. No more copy/paste of exercises.

2) “Ok, what do I do first?”

This is where things usually break. Glia shows you a clear agenda and helps you prioritise without guilt. It doesn’t say “do everything”. It says “pick one”.

📅 Clear agenda 🎯 Priority 🫶 Less fighting at home
Seeing tasks like this already lowers the noise.

3) “I still don’t get it” (without shame)

It doesn’t just spit out an answer. It walks you through levels: first the basics, then what matters, then what you need to move forward. The feeling changes: from “stuck” → “I know what to do”.

🧠 Scaffolded help 🗣️ Simple explanations ✅ Real progress
“I still don’t get it” → the help adapts, it doesn’t judge you.

4) When choosing is too much… Glia chooses with you

“Magic decision” exists for one specific day: the day when everything feels like too much. It proposes one doable action. Not perfect. Possible.

🪄 Magic Decision 🧩 Executable steps ⚡ Real energy
The goal is not perfection. The goal is to start.

And if today you really can’t… that still counts

Glia is not “toxic productivity”. It includes accessibility and wellbeing because learning also means self‑regulation. If you’re in crisis, you log it, breathe, and come back when you can.

♿ OpenDyslexic 🧘 Wellbeing ❤️ Less guilt
You’re not a robot. Studying is also about taking care of yourself.

Studying shouldn’t break you

If your reaction when you see homework is “I shut down”, Glia is built for you. It doesn’t promise miracles. It promises something better: a first step.
