The teacher’s challenge

“Adapted for home” always falls short

The issue isn’t lack of will. It’s lack of tools.

📚

Adapting multiplies work

One activity, 25 different minds. There aren’t enough hours in the day to manually personalise every worksheet.

🏠

Home is another world

What you work on in class loses coherence at home. Families don’t have the same context or toolkit you have.

🫣

The student doesn’t ask for help

They freeze, stay silent and show up without the homework done. You only find out they were stuck when it’s already too late.

How it works

From your worksheet to adapted steps in 4 clicks

You bring the material and make the pedagogical decisions. AI adapts. The student executes. You monitor.

1

Upload the material

PDF, photo of the book or worksheet. AI extracts the exercises with the full wording. No more copy/paste.

2

Choose class, profile and exercises

Select the class, cognitive profile (ADHD, autistic, stable, mixed) and which exercises to assign. You see in real time how many students will receive the task.

3

AI creates adapted steps

Gemini generates subtasks adjusted to each profile: very short for autistic students (4–6), clear for ADHD (3–4), normal for stable (3–5). Socratic method: guidance, not answers.

4

You review, edit and send

You see each task and subtask. You can edit, delete or add steps. Only when you approve it is it sent to the students’ agenda as required work.

Principles

What Glia doesn’t do is as important as what it does

🧭

AI guides, it doesn’t solve

Socratic method is non‑negotiable. Glia shows how to do each step, but never gives the answer. The goal is autonomy, not dependence.

👩‍🏫

It doesn’t replace the teacher

Glia always defers to a trusted adult or professional when the topic goes beyond cognitive support.

🔒

Real data protection

Teachers only see data from their students. Right to erasure implemented. Children’s data isn’t used to retrain models.

💚

Regulation before execution

If a student is in crisis, the app stops to focus on regulation first. No task is ever forced.

📉

If it creates dependence, it goes

The metric is that students need less Glia over time. More independence, not another layer of control.

🌍

5 languages

Spanish, Catalan, English, Basque and Galician. The full interface, not just a few buttons.

Classifier, Strategist, Simplifier and Reviewer work in a chain. The Reviewer checks answers against the school’s principles.

Coherence

Home + classroom = same language

Students and families see the same tasks with the same step‑by‑step structure. Fewer misunderstandings, fewer arguments.

Accessibility

High contrast, adjustable educational level (from primary to university) and voice support for students who cannot type.

Security

Strict roles. Encrypted data. Child‑protection protocols. Children’s data is never used to build generic “ed‑tech” models.
